How this works

A listing has to clear every hard requirement before it shows up anywhere on this site at all: rent between $4,000–$7,000, at least 1 bedroom, in-unit washer/dryer, and a kitchen that's open or semi-open (not closed or galley). Gowanus is excluded outright regardless of anything else. None of that is weighted or negotiable — it's pass/fail.

Everything that clears those gets a match score out of 100, blending three things:

  • Neighborhood preference — Upper West Side scores 100, Brooklyn scores 65, everywhere else (other Manhattan, Queens/LIC) scores 30.
  • Office commute — shorter is better, scored on a sliding scale.
  • Friends commute — averaged across your four friends (Upper West Side, Morningside Heights, LIC, Prospect Heights), same sliding scale.

The default weighting is 35% neighborhood, 35% office, 30% friends. Because the neighborhood tiers are far apart (100 vs. 65 vs. 30) and that's over a third of the score, a UWS listing starts with a real head start that a merely-better commute often can't fully close — that's not a bug, just what a 35% weight on a 70-point spread does. If that balance feels off, adjust it below; it re-sorts the New and All Qualifying tabs immediately and remembers your setting on this device.
