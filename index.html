How this works

A listing has to clear every hard requirement before it shows up anywhere on this site at all: rent between $4,000–$7,000, at least 1 bedroom, in-unit washer/dryer, and a kitchen that's open or semi-open (not closed or galley). Gowanus and Crown Heights are excluded outright regardless of anything else, and so is Upper West Side north of 96th Street — outside the comfort zone entirely, not just downweighted. None of that is weighted or negotiable — it's pass/fail.

Everything that clears those gets a match score out of 100, blending seven things:

  • Neighborhood preference — Brooklyn and Upper West Side in the 72nd–89th St sweet spot are tied at 100; UWS outside that range (but south of 96th) scores 80; everywhere else (other Manhattan, Queens/LIC) scores 30. Brooklyn and UWS tie deliberately: UWS's apparent edge turned out to be mostly proximity to a specific friend there, which the friends-commute score below already covers — holding commute fixed, UWS and Brooklyn are a genuine tie, not UWS-first. The 72nd-89th sweet spot inside UWS is a separate, real preference (not proximity-driven), so it stays as its own tier. Street cutoffs come from the actual geocoded address when it's on a numbered cross street ("West 94th Street"), or an estimate from the building's coordinates when it's on an avenue (Columbus, Amsterdam, West End, Central Park West) — StreetEasy only tags the whole stretch "Upper West Side," it doesn't say where in it a listing sits.
  • Office commute — shorter is better, scored on a sliding scale.
  • Friends commute — averaged across your four friends (Upper West Side, Morningside Heights, Hunters Point/LIC, Prospect Heights), same sliding scale.
  • Size — bigger is always better, but normalized by bedroom count (divided by the square root of bedrooms, so it's a real but weak adjustment, not a straight per-bedroom split) since the same square footage feels roomier with fewer bedrooms to share it — an 800sqft 1-bedroom is spacious, an 800sqft 2-bedroom is cramped by comparison. Below 600sqft per bedroom this goes negative instead of just bottoming out at 0, so a genuinely too-small unit actively drags the overall score down rather than merely failing to help it — otherwise a great location could paper over a unit that's just too small. Listings without sqft in the source data (most of them) get the median real sqft for that bedroom count instead of a guess or a flat default, so a missing value doesn't quietly become a penalty or a windfall.
  • Living room — binary for now (small or not, from the same photo-based check that already flags it elsewhere): a small living room scores 0, anything else scores 100. Came up repeatedly across directed listing reviews as the single biggest drag on an otherwise-good listing — entertaining space matters enough to weight it comparably to size, not just as a tiebreaker.
  • Kitchen size — also from the photo-based check, judging counter/cabinet run and floor space rather than open-vs-closed layout (that's a separate hard requirement above): large scores 100, small scores 0, standard or unclassifiable scores 50. Weighted lighter than the others (6%) since it's only come up once so far as an explicit callout, versus living room's four — real, but not yet a proven recurring pattern.
  • Condo — a boost, not a hard requirement: condos score 100, everything else (rental buildings, co-ops, mixed-use, multi-family) scores a neutral 50, so non-condos aren't penalized, condos just get lifted above the baseline. Detected directly from StreetEasy's own listing text. Added per explicit instruction rather than an observed review pattern, so weighted the same as kitchen size (6%) without waiting for repeat evidence.

The default weighting is 22% neighborhood, 22% office, 18% friends, 13% size, 13% living room, 6% kitchen size, 6% condo. If that balance feels off, adjust it below; it re-sorts the New and All Qualifying tabs immediately and remembers your setting on this device.
